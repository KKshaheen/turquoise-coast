Why here

The relaxing mix you wanted — coast on one side, nature on the other

The Fethiye–Göcek stretch is the gentlest corner of Turkey's coast: warm, swimmable turquoise bays, with pine-clad mountains, lagoons and gorges all within an easy drive. Calm enough to do nothing, varied enough that the girls never get bored.

About the flying part

You reach this coast through Dalaman airport — usually two shorter hops with one connection rather than one long haul. The final descent runs in along the calm Mediterranean shoreline, which tends to be one of the smoother approaches. We can choose the routing with the fewest, steadiest legs.

Where to stay

Two ways to do it

A full-service resort where the kids' clubs and beach do the work for you — or your own private villa with a pool, total privacy and family space. Tap to switch between them.

Kalkan is the coast's villa capital: white houses stacked up a hillside, most with their own private infinity pool looking out over the sea. Evenings are made for the harbour's rooftop restaurants, and beach clubs are a short hop by boat or taxi.

Quieter, greener villa country tucked into the pines around Göcek and Fethiye — handy for marinas and boat days, and a short drive from Ölüdeniz and the gorge. Ideal if you'd rather have a peaceful private base and explore by car.

Things to do

The days you'll remember

A handful of easy, family-friendly outings — most a short drive or boat ride from base. Pick a few; leave plenty of days for the pool.

Swim · half day

Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon

The postcard one: a calm, protected turquoise lagoon with shallow, safe water — ideal for the girls — plus sunbeds, pedalos and a long beach. Overhead, paragliders drift down from Babadağ mountain and land on the sand, which kids love to watch.

Go in the morning before it warms up and fills out.
Boat · full day

A boat day around Göcek's bays

Hire a private gulet for the day and drift between swimming-only coves — Bedri Rahmi bay with its painted rock, Cleopatra's Hammam, and pine-fringed Tersane island. Swim straight off the boat, lunch on board, no crowds.

A private boat means you set the pace and the swim stops.
Nature · half day

Saklıkent Gorge

A dramatic, narrow canyon you wade into through icy mountain water — wonderfully refreshing on a hot day. Restaurants sit on wooden platforms right over the river. Bring water shoes; the cold water is the whole point.

The natural air-conditioning day — pair it with a riverside lunch.

More family day trips

  • Dalyan & Iztuzu Turtle Beach — a gentle river-boat through reed channels, mud baths, and a long beach where loggerhead turtles nest.
  • Butterfly Valley — a short boat trip to a hidden valley beach ringed by cliffs.
  • Kayaköy ghost village — an easy wander through a hillside of stone houses, right by Hillside.
  • Watch the paragliders at Ölüdeniz — tandem flights for the brave; a great show for everyone else.
  • Tlos & Yakapark — Lycian hillside ruins and a spring-fed trout restaurant up in the cool mountains.
The week

A relaxed 7-night rhythm

Loose on purpose — busy days and pool days alternate, so no one's worn out. Easy to reshuffle around the weather and everyone's mood.

Day 1

Arrive & settle

Land at Dalaman, transfer to base, unpack and ease in with a first swim and dinner.

Day 2

Pure beach & pool

No agenda — settle into the place, find your spots, let the girls loose in the water.

Day 3

Göcek bays by boat

A full day on a private gulet, swimming between quiet coves with lunch on board.

Day 4

Ölüdeniz lagoon

Morning at the Blue Lagoon, paraglider-watching, then back for an afternoon rest.

Day 5

Slow day & spa

Lie low — spa, a long lunch, a wander through Göcek or Kalkan in the evening.

Day 6

Saklıkent Gorge

Cool off in the canyon and riverside lunch — or swap in the Dalyan turtle-beach trip.

Day 7

Last beach day

One more lazy day by the water, a final dinner, and an early pack.

Day 8

Fly home

Transfer to Dalaman and the easy two-leg hop back to Bahrain.

Getting there

Short, scenic, and over the sea

The whole region is served by Dalaman airport — close to Göcek and Fethiye, with a calm coastal approach to land on.

Two easy legs

From Bahrain you'll typically connect once (commonly via Istanbul) before the short final hop to Dalaman — two manageable flights instead of one long one. We'll pick the routing with the fewest, steadiest segments and best timings.

Then 30 minutes

Dalaman (DLM) sits about 30 minutes from Göcek and roughly 45 from Fethiye. A private car transfer through the hotel or villa makes the door-to-door part effortless.

Best window

Late July to mid-August is hot, dry and sunny, with warm, calm sea — peak beach weather. It's also peak season, so the best villas and rooms go early.

The gentle landing

The descent into Dalaman tracks the coastline rather than crossing mountains — usually one of the smoother approaches, which helps if the flying part is the nervy bit.
